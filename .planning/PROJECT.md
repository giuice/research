# Protocolos de Reabilitação Geriátrica Baseados em Evidências

## What This Is

A comprehensive set of evidence-based geriatric rehabilitation protocols covering the 20 most common comorbidities seen in specialized rehabilitation centers (representing ~80% of clinical presentations). Each protocol is individually researched in English from international literature, then written as a structured clinical document in Brazilian Portuguese. The project serves both clinical application and academic documentation.

## Core Value

Every protocol must be the most efficient "Protocol #1" for its comorbidity — prioritizing clinical outcomes, patient safety, and reduction of care-related risks (readmissions, post-operative complications), grounded in Level A evidence and international guidelines (IASP, AGS, WHO).

### Out of Scope

- Marketing or hospitality techniques — purely clinical-therapeutic focus
- Acute hospital care protocols — focus is on rehabilitation phases
- Pediatric or adult (non-geriatric) populations
- Surgical technique descriptions — only post-operative rehabilitation

## Context

- **Domain:** Geriatric Physical Therapy with Evidence-Based Practice (PBE) focus
- **Evidence standard:** Level A evidence aligned with IASP, AGS, WHO guidelines + RCTs and meta-analyses for specific dosages
- **Comorbidity list (20):**
  1. Osteoartrite / Artrose (joelho, quadril, coluna, mãos)
  2. Lombalgia Crônica e Estenose de Canal
  3. AVC/AVE (fase crônica, neuroplasticidade)
  4. Osteoporose (osteogênese e postura)
  5. Fratura de Fêmur Proximal / Quadril
  6. Doença de Parkinson
  7. Quedas e Síndrome Pós-Queda
  8. Sarcopenia / Fraqueza Generalizada
  9. Artrose de Coluna / Espondilose
  10. Pós-op Artroplastia Total de Joelho (ATJ)
  11. Pós-op Artroplastia Total de Quadril (ATQ)
  12. Dor Lombar Crônica Inespecífica
  13. Síndrome do Ombro Doloroso
  14. Disfunção do Assoalho Pélvico
  15. DPOC (reabilitação respiratória)
  16. Insuficiência Cardíaca / Cardiopatias
  17. Neuropatia Periférica (diabética)
  18. Demência / Alzheimer (leve a moderado)
  19. Fraturas Vertebrais por Compressão
  20. Gonalgia / Coxalgia Degenerativa
- **Approach:** Sequential — one comorbidity per phase, starting with Osteoarthritis. Earlier research informs clinically similar conditions later.
- **Phase structure per protocol:** Flexible (2-4 phases depending on clinical reality of each condition)
- **Exercise parameters:** Recommended values + acceptable ranges for clinical judgment

## Constraints

- **Language:** Research must be conducted in English for accuracy; final protocol documents in Brazilian Portuguese
- **Evidence level:** Minimum Level A evidence; lower levels only when A is unavailable, clearly noted
- **Sequential execution:** One comorbidity at a time to maintain depth and quality
