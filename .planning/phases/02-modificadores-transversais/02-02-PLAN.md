**Section-by-section specification:**

**Section 1 — Criterios Diagnosticos e de Inclusao:**
- WHO BMD T-score categories: Normal (>= -1.0), Osteopenia (-1.0 to -2.5), Osteoporose (<= -2.5)
- Osteoporose estabelecida = T-score <= -2.5 + fratura por fragilidade
- FRAX Brasil model for 10-year fracture probability (reference: sheffield.ac.uk/FRAX/tool.aspx?country=55)
- Inclusion: T-score <= -2.5 OR osteopenia with FRAX >= thresholds OR established osteoporosis, age >= 60
- Exclusion: active metastatic bone disease, acute unstable vertebral fracture (< 6 weeks), Paget's disease active phase

**Section 2 — Avaliacao Inicial Mandatoria:**
- TUG + SPPB-BR + Dinamometria Manual (inherited from SHARED-FOUNDATION)
- Avaliacao postural + cifose toracica (kyphosis measurement)
- FRAX calculation (Brasil model) — note: clinician uses online tool, protocol cannot substitute
- Height measurement (loss > 4 cm = suspect vertebral fracture)

**Section 3 — Alinhamento com Diretrizes:**
- "Too Fit to Fracture" 2014 (PubMed 25510579; PMC5112023)
- UK "Strong, Steady and Straight" 2022 (PMC9304091)
- BHOF/NOF Clinician's Guide 2022
- IOF/NOGG guidelines
- Explicit declaration per guideline

**Section 4 — Estrutura de Fases:**
- 3 phases: Fase 1 (Protecao e Adaptacao), Fase 2 (Fortalecimento e Carga Progressiva), Fase 3 (Manutencao e Autonomia Funcional)
- Functional entry/exit criteria
- Critical rule: fracture risk category governs exercise intensity ceiling throughout all phases

**Sections 5-7 — Intervencoes por Fase (Modalidade Terapeutica / Terapia Manual / Cinesioterapia):**
- Each phase with interventions under three categories
- Every exercise with two-column dosage table and 3 frailty rows
- Every intervention with Oxford CEBM evidence annotation
- **CRITICAL — Cross-Protocol Bone-Loading Constraint Table:** Use the template from 02-RESEARCH.md "Code Examples" section (Template: Bone-Loading Constraint Table). This table MUST be labeled with the "MODIFICADOR TRANSVERSAL — RESTRICOES DE CARGA OSSEA" blockquote. Include all 4 fracture risk categories (Baixo/Medio/Alto/Muito alto) with the 3 constraint axes (espinal/impacto/resistencia).
- Spinal extension exercises RECOMMENDED (Nivel B — Sinaki M et al. 1984, PubMed 6487063: 89% fracture rate in flexion group vs 16% in extension group)
- "Hip hinge" technique mandatory for all squats/lifts as universal precaution
- **Cross-reference P-08:** In kinesiotherapy sections, when prescribing resistance training, cite P-08's modifier table: "Dosagem de resistencia conforme P-08 — Tabela de Dosagem de Resistencia Estratificada por Fragilidade (Secao X)." Adapt P-08 parameters per bone-loading constraints.
- Include balance training (Tai Chi, static/dynamic balance) — connects to fall prevention
- Include impact exercise stratified by risk category per UK consensus
- Long-term HEP adherence strategies: progressive home program with 3-exercise minimum, pictographic instructions for low-literacy patients, monthly check-in schedule, family/caregiver engagement for very high risk

**Section 8 — Monitoramento de Dor:**
- NRS thresholds per phase (inherited format)
- Differentiation: acute fracture pain vs. musculoskeletal exercise soreness vs. new vertebral fracture pain (sudden onset thoracic/lumbar pain)

**Section 9 — Bandeiras Vermelhas e Escalonamento:**
- Red flag table with osteoporosis-specific entries (e.g., sudden height loss > 2 cm, new acute back pain, fall during session, pain after impact exercise)
- Escalation to orthopedist/rheumatologist

**Section 10 — Interacoes com Polifarmacia:**
- 6 SHARED-FOUNDATION classes + osteoporosis-specific: Bifosfonatos (timing — 30 min fasting before exercise not required but note GI effects), Denosumabe (ONJ risk awareness), SERMs (raloxifeno — VTE risk), Corticosteroides cronicos (bone loss acceleration — connect to P-08 miopatia), Suplementos calcio/vitamina D (dosage awareness, not PT prescription)
- Note: bisphosphonates + exercise are synergistic for BMD per BHOF 2022

**Section 11 — Precaucoes Cardiorrespiratorias:**
- Inherited from SHARED-FOUNDATION
- Note: many osteoporosis patients are on beta-blockers for cardiovascular comorbidities — use RPE

**Section 12 — Indicadores de Desfecho:**
- TUG (fall risk — target < 13.5 sec)
- SPPB-BR (balance — target >= 10)
- Dinamometria (grip strength maintenance/improvement)
- FRAX reavaliacao anual
- Height measurement (stability = no new vertebral fracture)
- Re-evaluation schedule table

**Section 13 — Rastreabilidade e Flags de Comorbidade:**
- Cross-reference flags: P-08 (resistance dosing), P-05 (femur fracture), P-07 (falls), P-09 (spinal OA), P-10 (ATJ), P-11 (ATQ), P-16 (heart failure — corticosteroid overlap), P-19 (vertebral fractures)

**Section 14 — Referencias por Nivel de Evidencia:**
- All citations by Nivel A/B/C
- Primary: "Too Fit to Fracture" (PubMed 25510579), UK consensus (PMC9304091), Sinaki 1984 (PubMed 6487063), BHOF/NOF 2022
- Secondary: PMC10345999, PMC8983944

**Section 15 — As 8 Questoes de Validade Clinica:**
- Same format as P-01 and P-08

**Writing requirements:**
- Entire document in Brazilian Portuguese
- Follow TIDieR/CERT reporting standards
- Match depth and rigor of P-01-OSTEOARTRITE.md and P-08-SARCOPENIA.md
- Target approximately 800-1200 lines
- Do NOT over-restrict exercise — evidence shows exercise groups have FEWER fractures (5.8%) than controls (9.6%). Use risk-stratified approach, never global restriction.
