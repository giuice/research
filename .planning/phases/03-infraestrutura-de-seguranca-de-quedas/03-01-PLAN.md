Create the complete P-07 Quedas e Sindrome Pos-Queda protocol following the established 15-section template from P-01, P-08, and P-04. Research phase is in English (PubMed, Cochrane, PEDro); document authorship is entirely in Brazilian Portuguese.

**15-section structure (follow template exactly):**

**Section 1 — Criterios Diagnosticos e de Inclusao:**
- WHO fall definition (involuntary event resulting in resting on ground/lower level)
- Post-fall syndrome as FULL TRIAD: (1) medo de cair (FES-I-BR >= 23), (2) restricao de atividade (avoidance behavior), (3) perda funcional progressiva (TUG deterioration, SPPB decline) — NOT just "fear of falling"
- Inclusion criteria: >= 1 fall in last 12 months OR TUG > 13.5 sec OR SPPB <= 9 OR FES-I >= 23 OR self-reported fear of falling
- Exclusion: acute untreated trauma, unstable fracture, severe uncontrolled orthostatic hypotension (systolic drop > 30 mmHg)

**Section 2 — Avaliacao Inicial Mandatoria:**
- TUG + SPPB-BR + Dinamometria Manual (inherited from SHARED-FOUNDATION)
- BBS-BR (Miyamoto 2004, PubMed 15334208): 14 items, 0-56 pts; fall risk cutoff < 45; MCID 3.3 pts (Donoghue 2009, conservative estimate, annotate Brazilian-specific MCID not confirmed)
- FES-I-BR (Camargos 2010, PubMed 20730369): 16 items, 16-64 pts; cutoffs >= 23 (sporadic), >= 31 (recurrent); Cronbach alpha = 0.93; ICC inter = 0.91; MCID 3 pts (clinical estimate, annotate)
- TSK-11-BR (Salvador 2020, PMC7990729): 11 items, 11-44 pts; Cronbach alpha = 0.77; ICC = 0.85; cutoff > 37 = clinically significant kinesiophobia (annotate: derived from chronic pain populations, not geriatric falls specifically)
- Kinesiophobia/fear-of-falling decision table (Pattern 3 from research): FES-I-BR + TSK-11-BR combined interpretation with clinical action thresholds

**Section 3 — Alinhamento com Diretrizes:**
- WFG 2022 (PubMed 36178003, PMC9523684) — primary
- AGS/BGS 2011 (PubMed 21226685)
- STEADI 2024 (PMC12303747)
- WFG AGS Response 2024 (PubMed 38131656)
- Oxford CEBM 2011 evidence grading (inherited)

**Section 4 — Estrutura de Fases:** 3 phases — Avaliacao e Adaptacao ao Movimento / Desafio de Equilibrio Progressivo / Equilibrio Avancado e Funcional

**Section 5 — Fase 1 (Aguda/Protegida):**
- Static balance training (Level 1 of hierarchy)
- Low-load functional kinesiotherapy
- **EMBED the session-level fall risk protocol as INFRAESTRUTURA DE SEGURANCA — PROTOCOLO DE RISCO DE QUEDA POR SESSAO reusable template block** with ALL 4 elements:
  1. VAS fatigue pre-session (<=4 proceed / 5-7 reduce 30% no perturbation / >=8 defer)
  2. Orthostatic BP check (supine 5 min -> stand 1 min + 3 min; threshold systolic >= 20 OR diastolic >= 10 mmHg; cite PMC10024337)
  3. TUG-anchored gait belt criteria table (<=10 optional / 10.1-13.5 recommended / 13.6-20 mandatory+therapist / >20 mandatory+parallel bars)
  4. Supervised exit protocol (5 min seated cooldown, dizziness check, assisted stand if TUG > 13.5, wait 2 min standing, do not release if dizzy/NRS>4/unstable, document VAS/BP/incidents)
- Label the session protocol block with cross-protocol citation format: "Aplicar Protocolo de Risco de Queda por Sessao conforme P-07, Secao 5.X."
- Two-column dosage tables (recommended value + acceptable clinical range) with 3 frailty rows (Robusto / Pre-fragil / Fragil)
- Function-based entry criteria and exit criteria to Phase 2

**Section 6 — Fase 2 (Subaguda/Fortalecimento):**
- Dynamic balance + functional gait training (Level 2 of hierarchy)
- Introduction of dual-task training (gradual, Level 3 elements)
- Strength training cross-referencing P-08 Secao 6.3 for resistance dosing in sarcopenic fallers
- Two-column dosage tables with 3 frailty rows
- Function-based progression criteria

**Section 7 — Fase 3 (Avancada/Funcional):**
- Advanced dual-task training (Level 3)
- Perturbation-based training (Level 4) — include BOTH treadmill-PBT (gold standard, harness mandatory) AND manual perturbation alternatives (therapist-applied, foam surface) for settings without treadmill
- **EMBED the balance intervention hierarchy as INFRAESTRUTURA DE SEGURANCA — HIERARQUIA DE INTERVENCAO DE EQUILIBRIO cross-protocol reference table** using Pattern 1 from research:
  - 4 levels (Estatico / Dinamico / Dupla Tarefa / Perturbacao)
  - Each level: TUG/SPPB/BBS entry criteria, exit criteria, typical dosage, frailty note
  - Frailty override: Fragil (Fried >= 3) always start Level 1 regardless of TUG
  - Label with blockquote callout naming downstream protocols (P-03, P-05, P-06, P-08, P-10, P-11, P-18) and citation format: "Progressao de equilibrio conforme P-07 — Tabela de Hierarquia de Intervencao de Equilibrio (Secao 7.X)."
- Perturbation training safety: harness mandatory (PMC10587291); pre-test 1-3 min without perturbations; frail patients eligible with safeguards (61% of pilot participants were frail)
- Two-column dosage tables with 3 frailty rows

**Section 8 — Monitoramento de Dor:** NRS thresholds per phase (inherited template from P-01)

**Section 9 — Bandeiras Vermelhas e Escalonamento:** Use Pattern 4 from research — falls-specific red flag table including: fall during session, syncope/pre-syncope, persistent dizziness > 5 min, new neurological deficit, sudden severe headache, chest pain/dyspnea, suspected new fracture, acute confusion (delirium)

**Section 10 — Interacoes com Polifarmacia:** 6 SHARED-FOUNDATION base classes + 6 falls-specific classes from research: Benzodiazepinicos/Z-drugs, Anti-hipertensivos (alfa-bloqueadores, diureticos), Antidepressivos triciclicos/ISRS, Antiepilepticos (gabapentina, pregabalina), Opioides, Antipsicoticos. Include STOPPFall reference for medication review escalation.

**Section 11 — Precaucoes Cardiorrespiratorias:** Inherited template (placeholder for Phase 6 retroactive update)

**Section 12 — Indicadores de Desfecho:**
- TUG (meta < 13.5 sec; MCID 2-3 sec)
- SPPB-BR (meta >= 10; MCID 1.0 pt)
- BBS-BR (meta >= 45; MCID 3.3 pts — cite Donoghue 2009; annotate Brazilian-specific MCID unconfirmed)
- FES-I-BR (meta < 23; MCID 3 pts)
- Fall frequency (self-report + fall diary)
- TSK-11-BR (reduction target, not used as primary outcome)
- Re-evaluation schedule: 4 weeks (within-phase), phase transition, discharge

**Section 13 — Rastreabilidade e Flags de Comorbidade:** Cross-reference to P-08 (sarcopenia/weakness), P-04 (osteoporosis), P-05 (hip fracture), P-06 (Parkinson), P-03 (stroke), P-10 (TKA), P-11 (THA), P-18 (dementia). Use modifier chain: P-04 = bone-loading ceiling, P-08 = resistance floor, P-07 = balance progression + session safety.

**Section 15 — As 8 Questoes de Validade Clinica:** Answer all 8 questions with section cross-references (who qualifies, when to stop, where to start, what to do, how much, how fast, how to measure progress, why these choices). Follow exact format from P-01 and P-08.

**Critical anti-patterns to AVOID (from research):**
- Do NOT define post-fall syndrome as only "fear of falling" — must be the full triad
- Do NOT create balance hierarchy without TUG/SPPB/BBS numerical entry criteria for each level
- Do NOT exclude frail patients from perturbation training — include with safeguards
- Do NOT write session protocol as narrative prose — must be decision-table format with thresholds
- Do NOT use FES-I-BR cutoffs without citing Camargos 2010 specifically
- Do NOT omit the cross-protocol modifier labels (INFRAESTRUTURA DE SEGURANCA blockquote callouts)
- Do NOT use time-based progression — function-based criteria only (DOSE-04)
- Do NOT use Tinetti scale as primary balance outcome — use BBS-BR
