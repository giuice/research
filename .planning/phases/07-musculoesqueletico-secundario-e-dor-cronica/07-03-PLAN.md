--- P-13 SINDROME DO OMBRO DOLOROSO ---

Critical content requirements:
- Section 1: Diagnostic criteria differentiated BY MECHANISM — this is the key structural decision for P-13:
  (a) Subacromial impingement syndrome (most common in elderly — positive Neer/Hawkins)
  (b) Adhesive capsulitis / frozen shoulder (global ROM restriction, 3 clinical stages)
  (c) Rotator cuff tendinopathy (partial/complete tears — note: asymptomatic tears are HIGHLY prevalent in elderly per 2020-2024 meta-analyses; surgery not indicated for asymptomatic tears with preserved function)
  (d) Mixed/combined presentation
  NAO COBRE: P-01 or P-09 for spinal OA; P-10/P-11 for post-arthroplasty shoulder; cervical radiculopathy (different mechanism).
- Section 2: Universal battery + DASH-BR (Orfale 2005, PubMed 15821858) as primary outcome. SPADI-BR (Martins 2010, PubMed 20802993) as alternative. NRS. CSI-BR when chronic shoulder pain > 6 months with inadequate response. Goniometric ROM assessment (flexion, abduction, external/internal rotation).
- Section 3: AAOS CPG Rotator Cuff 2019, JOSPT CPG Shoulder 2013 (adhesive capsulitis), NICE NG228.
- Sections 4-7: Three phases adapted BY MECHANISM (different entry criteria and progression for capsulite vs. impingement vs. tendinopathy):
  - Phase 1: Pain control, protected ROM progression (respect muscular guarding), modality-specific approach (capsulite: passive stretching within tolerance; impingement: scapular stabilization + posture; tendinopathy: isometric loading).
  - Phase 2: Progressive strengthening — scapular stabilizers, rotator cuff (especially external rotators), progressive loading protocol. Eccentric training for tendinopathy (Level A evidence).
  - Phase 3: Functional integration — overhead activities, AVD simulation, return to full ROM maintenance.
  - Frailty-stratified dosage tables. Cross-reference P-08 Section 6.3 for resistance dosing. Cross-reference P-04 when osteoporosis coexists (shoulder exercises near overhead may need bone-loading modifier for concurrent vertebral osteoporosis).
- Section 8: Pain monitoring (NRS).
- Section 9: Red flags — complete rotator cuff tear with acute weakness (refer to orthopedics), glenohumeral instability, referred cardiac pain (left shoulder — differentiate from MSK), cervical myelopathy signs.
- Section 10: Polypharmacy — corticosteroid injections (as exercise window facilitator, not standalone treatment per ACP/NICE), NSAIDs topical vs. oral, AGS Beers.
- Section 11: Universal cardiorespiratory template verbatim + adapted subsections.
- Section 12: Outcome indicators — DASH-BR (MCID 10-14 points), SPADI-BR (MCID 13 points), NRS, ROM (flexion, abduction, ER), SPPB-BR, TUG.
- Section 13: Comorbidity flags.
- Section 14: References by CEBM level.
- Section 15: 8 clinical validity questions.
- PNE as ADJUNCT when CSI >= 40 or chronic pain > 6 months.
- CSI: apply when chronic shoulder pain > 6 months — if >= 40, time-contingent progression.

--- P-20 GONALGIA/COXALGIA DEGENERATIVA ---

Critical content requirements:
- Section 1: POPULATION DIFFERENTIATION from P-01 is the defining feature. Explicit statement: "Este protocolo cobre pacientes com gonalgia/coxalgia degenerativa que permanecem em manejo conservador DEFINITIVO — sem indicacao cirurgica, recusam cirurgia, ou possuem alto risco cirurgico. Para OA em processo de decisao pre-cirurgica ou com indicacao cirurgica ainda em aberto, ver P-01."
  NAO COBRE: P-01 if still in surgical decision; P-10 if post-ATJ; P-11 if post-ATQ; P-05 if post-hip-fracture.
  The therapeutic difference: P-20 emphasizes LONG-TERM FUNCTIONAL MAINTENANCE, expectation management, and permanent AVD adaptations. P-01 emphasizes pre-operative optimization.
- Section 2: Universal battery + WOMAC-BR (Oliveira 2022, PMC9673866) as PRIMARY (already confirmed). KOOS-BR (knee) and HOOS-BR (hip) as secondary with validation caution note. NRS. CSI-BR when chronic pain with central features. P-08 sarcopenia screen mandatory. P-04 bone-loading modifier when T-score indicates.
- Section 3: OARSI 2019 (same as P-01 but with conservative management emphasis), NICE NG226, AGS OA recommendations.
- Sections 4-7: Three phases — but Phase 3 structure DIFFERS from P-01:
  - Phase 1: Functional pain control, gentle ROM, isometric quadriceps/gluteal activation, aquatic exercise (equal first-line per OARSI 2019 — already established in P-01).
  - Phase 2: Progressive land-based strengthening, proprioceptive training, gait pattern optimization. Dosage tables with P-08 modifier when sarcopenia coexists.
  - Phase 3: LONG-TERM MAINTENANCE (differs from P-01 — P-20 Phase 3 is permanent, not a transition to surgical decision). Self-managed HEP, community exercise integration, periodic reassessment (quarterly), expectation management for chronic condition, permanent AVD adaptations (assistive devices acceptance, home modification guidance).
  - Frailty-stratified dosage tables. Cross-reference P-04, P-07, P-08 where indicated.
- Section 8: Pain monitoring (NRS).
- Section 9: Red flags — acute joint effusion with warmth (septic arthritis rule-out), sudden mechanical locking (loose body), rapid functional decline (may indicate need to reconsider surgical option = refer back to P-01 pathway).
- Section 10: Polypharmacy — topical NSAIDs (preferred over oral per OARSI 2019 for elderly), paracetamol, intra-articular hyaluronic acid awareness, AGS Beers for opioids.
- Section 11: Universal cardiorespiratory template verbatim + adapted subsections.
- Section 12: WOMAC-BR (MCID varies by domain), NRS, SPPB-BR, TUG, 30-Second Sit-to-Stand.
- Section 13: Comorbidity flags.
- Section 14: References by CEBM level.
- Section 15: 8 clinical validity questions — must confirm P-20's Phase 4 structure is DIFFERENT from P-01.
- PNE as COMPONENT (chronic pain education, expectation management).
- CSI when chronic pain with central features — if >= 40, adapt progression.
