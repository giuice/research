This protocol is the OPPOSITE POLE of P-02: defined by ABSENCE of structural substrate sufficient to explain pain burden. IASP Chronic Primary Pain (ICD-11 MG30.0) is the diagnostic anchor. PNE is the CENTRAL component (not adjunct as in P-02/P-09).

Critical content requirements:
- Section 1: Diagnostic criteria — Chronic low back pain > 3 months WITHOUT structural diagnosis explaining pain severity. IASP Chronic Primary Pain criteria (ICD-11 MG30.0) as anchor. Imaging NOT routinely recommended (NICE NG59). NAO COBRE block: P-02 if stenosis/claudication confirmed; P-09 if spondylosis with mechanical pattern; P-19 if compression fracture. At least 3 objectively distinct diagnostic criteria from P-02.
- Section 2: Universal battery + ODI-BR (primary), NRS, PSFS-BR (caution), CSI-BR MANDATORY at initial assessment (not optional like P-02/P-09), STarT Back (mandatory — stratify low/medium/high risk), HADS-BR mandatory (anxiety >= 11 or depression >= 11 = psychological referral before progression). Include CSI action plan table: 0-29 standard, 30-39 add PNE-1, 40-49 PNE mandatory + time-contingent progression, >= 50 PNE + psychological evaluation + pain medicine coordination.
- Section 3: NICE NG261 (2024) as primary (confirm status during research), NICE NG59, ACP 2017, IASP 2021 Classification.
- Sections 4-7: Three phases with biopsychosocial approach:
  - Phase 1: Pain control + PNE sessions 1-2 (neuromatrix, sensitization concepts) + gentle graded activity. NO passive modalities as primary intervention (NICE NG261). Functional goals set by PSFS-BR.
  - Phase 2: PNE session 3 + graded exposure to feared movements + progressive core strengthening. If CSI >= 40: time-contingent progression (increase load by time intervals, NOT by pain absence). Movement confidence scale (0-10) as progression criterion when NRS is dominant.
  - Phase 3: Functional integration + self-management strategies + long-term maintenance HEP + relapse prevention plan. PNE reinforcement as needed.
  - Frailty-stratified dosage tables in all phases.
  - Cross-reference P-08 Section 6.3 for resistance dosing. Cross-reference P-07 when TUG > 13.5s.
- PNE STRUCTURED DOSAGE (this is the differentiator from P-02/P-09):
  - 3-4 formal sessions of 30-40 minutes each
  - Module 1: What is pain? Neuromatrix; pain != tissue damage; car alarm analogy
  - Module 2: Central sensitization; how the nervous system "amplifies"; factors that increase the alarm
  - Module 3: Context and movement; contextual factors (stress, sleep, catastrophizing); safe return to activities
  - Module 4 (optional): Relapse prevention; self-management; when to seek help
  - Reference: Louw A et al. Pain Physician 2016; Moseley GL & Butler DS 2015
  - PNE must occupy at least 15-20 lines of structured content — NOT a brief mention
- Section 8: Pain monitoring — critical note: in patients with CSI >= 40, NRS is NOT a reliable indicator of tissue damage. Time-contingent progression replaces pain-contingent progression.
- Section 9: Red flags (cauda equina, progressive neurological deficit) + YELLOW FLAGS section (psychosocial flags: catastrophizing PCS-BR >= 30, fear-avoidance, depression HADS >= 11, work-related factors, passive coping). Yellow flags trigger PNE reinforcement + psychological referral pathway.
- Section 10: Polypharmacy — SPECIAL SECTION on opioids in nonspecific chronic pain (highest risk of inappropriate chronic use per AGS Beers 2023). Tramadol/codeine explicitly flagged. Gabapentinoids with sedation/fall risk. NSAIDs with GI/renal risk in elderly. Multimodal non-opioid approach preferred.
- Section 11: Universal cardiorespiratory template verbatim (11.1, 11.2) + adapted 11.3, 11.4.
- Section 12: Outcome indicators — ODI-BR (MCID >= 10), NRS (note limitation in central sensitization), CSI (reduction from baseline), PCS-BR (reduction in catastrophizing), PSFS-BR (MCID 2 points per activity), SPPB-BR, TUG.
- Section 13: Comorbidity flags.
- Section 14: References by CEBM level.
- Section 15: 8 clinical validity questions.

This is the HIGHEST SAFETY RISK protocol in Phase 7. Osteoporosis is the substrate in >80% of vertebral compression fractures in elderly. P-04 Section 5.2 (bone-loading constraints) GOVERNS all exercise prescription without exception.

Critical content requirements:
- Section 1: Diagnostic criteria — vertebral compression fracture confirmed by imaging (X-ray or CT/MRI). Differentiate from P-04 (P-19 = acute/subacute fracture rehabilitation; P-04 = chronic osteoporosis management and fracture prevention). NAO COBRE: P-04 after rehab discharge; P-02 if stenosis/claudication without fracture; P-09 if spondylosis without fracture. Include explicit statement: "Este protocolo co-gerencia P-04. Todas as cargas de exercicio sao limitadas pela Tabela MODIFICADOR TRANSVERSAL de P-04 Secao 5.2."
- Section 2: Universal battery + ODI-BR (primary), NRS, FES-I-BR (mandatory — fear of falling post-fracture), BBS-BR (when TUG > 13.5s), FRAX + T-score from P-04 (mandatory to classify bone-loading category). CSI when persistent pain > 3 months beyond expected structural recovery.
- Section 3: NOF/BHOF 2022, NICE CG146 (2012/2023), P-04 as cross-protocol modifier reference.
- Sections 4-7: Three phases:
  - Phase 1 (Acute/Protected): Conservative regime with bracing if indicated by orthopedist. Progressive mobilization (bed mobility → sit → stand → gait). Vertebral FLEXION CONTRAINDICATED (Sinaki 1984, PubMed 6487063 — extension > flexion rule in osteoporosis applies with even greater force here). Isometric trunk extension only. Pain-limited ROM. Hip hinge mandatory for all lift/squat movements. Impact exercises CONTRAINDICATED in all phases for High and Very High Risk categories (P-04 Section 5.2).
  - Phase 2 (Subacute/Strengthening): Isometric → isotonic progressive extension. Paraspinal strengthening. Postural re-education. Weight-bearing progression per orthopedist clearance. Dosage table must include "Teto P-04" column for each exercise showing bone-loading ceiling.
  - Phase 3 (Advanced/Functional): Functional integration, balance training (P-07 hierarchy), fall prevention, long-term HEP with bone-health maintenance. Continue hip hinge as permanent movement pattern.
  - Frailty-stratified dosage tables with additional "Teto P-04" column per exercise.
  - Cross-reference P-08 Section 6.3 for resistance dosing.
  - Cross-reference P-07 Section 7.2 for balance hierarchy and session fall risk protocol (mandatory — post-fracture patients are highest fall risk).
- Section 8: Pain monitoring — note that persistent pain > 3 months may indicate new fracture, non-union, or central sensitization.
- Section 9: Red flags — CAUDA EQUINA SYNDROME as maximum emergency (urinary retention + saddle anesthesia = SAMU 192 + DO NOT MOBILIZE). New neurological deficit. Sudden increase in pain suggesting new fracture. Vertebral instability signs.
- Section 10: Polypharmacy — calcium/vitamin D, bisphosphonates, denosumab awareness (not prescribing — awareness for exercise timing). Analgesic management. AGS Beers for opioids post-fracture.
- Section 11: Universal cardiorespiratory template verbatim + adapted subsections.
- Section 12: Outcome indicators — ODI-BR (MCID >= 10), NRS, SPPB-BR, TUG, FES-I-BR, number of new fractures (safety outcome).
- Section 13: Comorbidity flags — P-04 integration is PRIMARY. P-07 for fall prevention. P-08 for sarcopenia.
- Section 14: References by CEBM level.
- Section 15: 8 clinical validity questions.
- PNE as COMPONENT (education on post-fracture pain, not central like P-12).
