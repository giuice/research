Create the complete P-05 Fratura de Femur Proximal protocol following the established 15-section template from P-01, P-08, P-04, and P-07. Research phase is in English (PubMed, Cochrane, PEDro); document authorship is entirely in Brazilian Portuguese.

**15-section structure (follow template exactly):**

**Section 1 — Criterios Diagnosticos e de Inclusao:**
- Fracture classification: intracapsular (subcapital, transcervical) vs. extracapsular (intertrocanteriana, subtrocanteriana)
- Surgical fixation types: osteossintese (DHS, haste intramedular) vs. artroplastia parcial (hemiartroplastia) vs. artroplastia total (encaminhar subcaso P-11 se ATQ eletiva)
- Inclusion: age >= 60, post-surgical fixation for proximal femur fracture, admitted to rehabilitation program
- Exclusion: pathological fracture from metastatic disease, periprosthetic fracture (refer orthopedics), polytrauma with multi-organ involvement, CFS 9 (terminal)
- Scope statement: P-05 covers post-hip-fracture rehabilitation. NOT elective THA (see P-11). NOT periprosthetic fracture.

**Section 2 — Avaliacao Inicial Mandatoria:**
- TUG + SPPB-BR + Dinamometria Manual (inherited SHARED-FOUNDATION)
- CFS (Rockwood 2005, CMAJ) — MANDATORY at admission for P-05. CFS predicts 30-day mortality (1.3% CFS 1-3 vs. 14.6% CFS >= 7) and 1-year mortality (3.8% vs. 41.7%). Source: PubMed 33215137. Mapping: CFS 1-3 = Robusto; CFS 4-5 = Pre-fragil; CFS 6-7 = Fragil; CFS 8 = abordagem paliativa conjunta; CFS 9 = fora do escopo.
- Complementar com Fenotipo de Fried completo quando tempo disponivel. CFS define a linha de entrada na tabela de dosagem.
- BBS-BR (Miyamoto 2004, PubMed 15334208): mandatory — high fall risk population
- FES-I-BR (Camargos 2010, PubMed 20730369): mandatory — post-fracture fear of falling screening, activates P-07 co-modifier when >= 23
- MoCA-BR: cognitive screening for delirium/dementia assessment (common post-hip-fracture)
- Weight-bearing status: document surgeon's clearance (WBAT / partial WB / NWB) — this drives Phase 1 exercise selection

**Section 3 — Alinhamento com Diretrizes:**
- AAOS CPG Management of Hip Fractures in Older Adults 2021 (PubMed 36200817; PMC6948785)
- APTA CPG Physical Therapy Management Older Adults with Hip Fracture 2021 (JOSPT 2021;51(2))
- IGFS Consensus Post-Acute Care of Hip Fracture 2021 (PMC7366407)
- Oxford CEBM 2011 evidence grading (inherited)

**Section 4 — Estrutura de Fases:** 3 phases:
- Fase 1: Aguda/Protegida (0-6 semanas pos-op ou admissao reabilitacao)
- Fase 2: Subaguda/Fortalecimento (6-12 semanas)
- Fase 3: Avancada/Funcional (> 12 semanas)
Note: temporal references are MINIMUM safety windows, not progression criteria. Progression is always function-based (DOSE-04).

**Section 5 — Fase 1 (Aguda/Protegida):**
- Mobilization < 48h post-op (AAOS 2021 recommendation — also mechanical VTE prophylaxis)
- WBAT as default when surgically cleared — do NOT wait for radiological consolidation
- Bed mobility, transfers, sit-to-stand with assistive device
- Ankle pumps + quadriceps isometrics + gluteal sets (VTE prevention + muscle activation)
- Gait training with walker/crutches as tolerated
- Static balance training (Level 1 of P-07 hierarchy) — cite P-07 Secao 7.2
- Apply P-07 Session Fall Risk Protocol — cite P-07 Secao 5.2
- Bone-loading constraints from P-04 Secao 5.2 apply (osteoporosis is frequent substrate of fragility fracture)
- Two-column dosage tables (recommended value + acceptable clinical range) with 3 frailty rows (Robusto / Pre-fragil / Fragil)
- CFS-anchored intensity: CFS 1-3 intensive rehab; CFS 4-5 moderate intensity increased supervision; CFS 6-7 conservative start interdisciplinary mandatory
- Function-based exit criteria to Phase 2: independent bed mobility, supervised transfers, ambulation >= 15m with device, TUG improvable (baseline established)

**Section 6 — Fase 2 (Subaguda/Fortalecimento):**
- Progressive resistance training — cite P-08 Secao 6.3 for frailty-stratified dosing
- Dynamic balance training (Level 2 of P-07 hierarchy)
- Functional gait training (varied surfaces, obstacles, stairs when safe)
- Hip strengthening (abductors, extensors) — critical for fracture-side recovery
- ADL training progression
- Bone-loading constraints maintained (P-04 Secao 5.2)
- Two-column dosage tables with 3 frailty rows
- Function-based exit criteria: TUG < 20 sec, SPPB >= 7, independent transfers, ambulation >= 50m with/without device

**Section 7 — Fase 3 (Avancada/Funcional):**
- Advanced functional training
- Dual-task training (Level 3 of P-07 hierarchy)
- Community mobility and fall prevention integration
- Progressive resistance per P-08 Secao 6.3
- Introduction to Level 4 (perturbation) if criteria met and harness available (cite P-07 Secao 7.2)
- HEP establishment for long-term fall prevention
- Two-column dosage tables with 3 frailty rows
- Discharge criteria: TUG < 13.5 sec (or best achievable with documented plateau), SPPB >= 9, BBS >= 45, FES-I-BR < 23 (or improving trend), independent community ambulation

**Section 8 — Monitoramento de Dor:** NRS thresholds per phase (inherited template from P-01). Note surgical site pain vs. new pain differentiation.

**Section 9 — Bandeiras Vermelhas e Escalonamento:**
- All general red flags (SHARED-FOUNDATION Secao 7.2)
- P-05 specific: signs of non-union (persistent pain > 6 weeks with weight-bearing, inability to progress despite compliance), surgical site infection (erythema, warmth, purulent drainage, fever > 38C), active VTE symptoms (calf swelling, Homans sign — although low specificity, warmth, dyspnea/chest pain for PE), acute confusion/delirium (common post-hip-fracture, especially CFS >= 5), new fall during rehabilitation
- Escalation table with action (suspend session / contact physician / emergency) per red flag

**Section 10 — Interacoes com Polifarmacia:**
- 6 SHARED-FOUNDATION base classes

- TEV Awareness section (use exact pattern from research Code Examples):

  - AAOS 2021 CPG reference for context
  - Table: anticoagulant agent | exercise implication | modification (LMWH, DOACs, Aspirina, Warfarina)
  - Mobilization precoce as mechanical VTE prophylaxis
- Additional: Opioides (frequent post-fracture, exercise timing), Corticosteroides

**Section 11 — Precaucoes Cardiorrespiratorias:** Inherited template (placeholder for Phase 6 retroactive update)

**Section 12 — Indicadores de Desfecho:**

**Section 13 — Rastreabilidade e Flags de Comorbidade:**
Populate by citation — NEVER re-derive:

- Protocolo de Sessao: cite P-07 Secao 5.2 (INFRAESTRUTURA DE SEGURANCA) — applicable all sessions
- Flag: "Ativar P-07 como comodificador obrigatorio" when FES-I-BR >= 23

**Section 14 — Referencias por Nivel de Evidencia:** Organize all citations by Oxford CEBM level. Include: AAOS 2021, APTA 2021, IGFS 2021, Rockwood CFS 2005, PubMed 33215137 (CFS mortality), PMC6394234 (early mobilization), SHARED-FOUNDATION sources.

**Section 15 — As 8 Questoes de Validade Clinica:** Answer all 8 questions with section cross-references. Follow exact format from P-01, P-08, P-04, P-07.
