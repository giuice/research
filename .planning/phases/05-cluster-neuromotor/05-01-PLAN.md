**Section-by-section requirements (all in Brazilian Portuguese):**

**Section 1 — Criterios Diagnosticos e de Inclusao:**
- AVC isquemico ou hemorragico fase cronica (> 6 meses do evento)
- Hemiparesia/plegia residual com potencial de reabilitacao
- Em acompanhamento neurologico
- Exclusao: AVC < 6 meses (encaminhar neuroreabilitacao aguda/subaguda), AVC de tronco com instabilidade vital, demencia grave associada (MMSE < 10 — ver P-18)
- Scope block: NÃO COBRE AVC agudo/subagudo, traumatismo cranioencefalico, lesao medular

**Section 2 — Avaliacao Inicial Mandatoria:**
- Bateria universal herdada: TUG + SPPB-BR + Dinamometria Manual (SHARED-FOUNDATION Secoes 1.1-1.3)
- BBS-BR (Miyamoto 2004; PubMed 15334208) — mandatory for neuromotor fall risk
- FES-I-BR (Camargos 2010; PubMed 20730369) — fear of falling screening
- MoCA-BR (Memoria 2013; PubMed 22368034) — cognitive screening (post-stroke cognitive impairment is common)
- Escala de Ashworth Modificada — spasticity grading per affected limb segment
- FMA-MMII (Fugl-Meyer subescala MMII; Michaelsen 2011) — motor impairment baseline and outcome
- Avaliacao de afasia — document type (expressiva/receptiva/mista/global) and severity for instruction adaptation
- Avaliacao de apraxia de membro — ability to imitate gestures and use objects

**Section 3 — Alinhamento com Diretrizes:**
- AHA/ASA 2016 (PubMed 27145936) — primary guideline
- VA/DoD 2024 (PubMed 39832369) — most recent update with 47 recommendations
- WSO as epidemiological context
- Oxford CEBM classification for all evidence levels

**Section 4 — Estrutura de Fases (3 functional phases):**
- Fase 1: Aguda/Protegida — assessment, instruction adaptation, low load, session safety (P-07 Secao 5.2)
- Fase 2: Subaguda/Fortalecimento — progressive functional load, task-specific training
- Fase 3: Avancada/Funcional — dual-task training (TEMPLATE CLUSTER NEUROMOTOR), autonomy, HEP
- All phase transitions use function-based criteria (BBS-BR, TUG, SPPB-BR thresholds) with temporal minimums — never time-only

**Sections 5-7 — Phase content:**
Each phase section MUST include:
- Entry/exit criteria with specific instrument thresholds (checklist format)
- Interventions classified by: Modalidade Terapeutica, Terapia Manual, Cinesioterapia
- Two-column dosage tables (recommended + acceptable range) with 3 frailty rows (Robusto/Pre-fragil/Fragil)
- Neuroplasticity principle declaration: which Kleim & Jones principles are activated in this phase and WHY

Phase-specific content:
- Phase 1 (Section 5): Neuroplasticity foundation declaration (10 principles, Kleim & Jones 2008 PMID 18230848). Instruction adaptation subsection for aphasia/apraxia: physical demonstration as primary instruction, hand-over-hand guidance, visual feedback (mirror, camera), single-keyword verbal commands, communication with speech therapy. Static balance (P-07 Section 7.2 Level 1). Session safety protocol (P-07 Section 5.2).
- Phase 2 (Section 6): Task-specific practice (AHA/ASA 2016 Level A). Mirror therapy (VA/DoD 2024 endorsed). Constraint-induced movement therapy principles where applicable. Dynamic balance (P-07 Section 7.2 Level 2). Resistance training per P-08 Section 6.3 cross-reference.
- Phase 3 (Section 7): TEMPLATE CLUSTER NEUROMOTOR — Dual-Task Training. This template MUST be created here as a formal, named, referenceable section. Include: evidence (PMC12261537 meta-analysis 30 RCTs), entry criteria (BBS-BR >= 40 + TUG < 20 + 2 sessions without balance events), 4-level progressive difficulty table (DT-1 through DT-4), dosage table with frailty rows, and cognitive adaptation for aphasia (substitute verbal task with visual task). Community reintegration. HEP design.

**Section 8 — Monitoramento de Dor:**
- NRS 0-10 as standard when verbal communication preserved
- PAINAD-BR (Morello 2015; PubMed 25993063) as PRIMARY instrument when severe aphasia or receptive aphasia makes NRS unreliable
- Include PAINAD 5-domain table and action thresholds (0-2 continue, 3-5 reduce 25%, >= 6 suspend)

**Section 9 — Bandeiras Vermelhas:**
- Inherited general red flags (SHARED-FOUNDATION Section 7.2)
- P-03 specific: new focal neurological deficit, sudden severe headache (possible recurrent stroke), deterioration of consciousness level, sudden increase in spasticity pattern (possible new lesion)

**Section 10 — Interacoes com Polifarmacia:**
- Anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs) — bleeding risk with falls, INR monitoring awareness
- Antihypertensives — orthostatic hypotension risk during position changes
- Antidepressants (SSRIs) — commonly prescribed post-stroke; hyponatremia risk, fall risk
- Antispastics (baclofen) — sedation affecting exercise tolerance; tizanidine similar
- Anticonvulsants — sedation, ataxia
- STOPPFall reference for comprehensive screening

**Section 11 — Precaucoes Cardiorrespiratorias (inherited SHARED-FOUNDATION Section 7.4)**

**Section 12 — Indicadores de Desfecho:**
- FMA-MMII (motor impairment), BBS-BR (balance), TUG (mobility/fall risk), SPPB-BR (physical performance), 10-meter walk test (gait speed)
- Re-evaluation schedule aligned with phase transitions

**Section 13 — Rastreabilidade e Flags de Comorbidade:**
- P-07 Secao 5.2 (session safety protocol) — mandatory for ALL patients
- P-07 Secao 7.2 (balance hierarchy) — entry based on BBS-BR score
- P-08 Secao 6.3 (resistance dosing) — when sarcopenia co-exists (dynamometry screening)
- P-04 Secao 5.2 (bone-loading constraints) — when osteoporosis co-exists (T-score data)
- Comorbidity flags: P-01 (if OA co-exists), P-05 (if prior hip fracture), P-06 (if Parkinson co-exists), P-18 (if cognitive impairment beyond post-stroke)
- ALL cross-references cite specific section numbers — zero re-derivation

**Section 15 — As 8 Questoes de Validade Clinica:**
- Answer all 8 questions established in P-01 template (who qualifies, when to stop, where to start, what to do, how much, how fast, how to measure progress, why these choices)
