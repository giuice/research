## P-06 PARKINSON — Section-by-section requirements:

**Section 1 — Criterios Diagnosticos e de Inclusao:**
- Doenca de Parkinson confirmada por criterios UKPDSBB; Hoehn and Yahr 1.5-4
- Em uso de levodopa ou agonistas dopaminergicos
- Exclusao: parkinsonismos atipicos sem diagnostico confirmado (PSP, MSA, DCL), Hoehn and Yahr 5 (acamado)
- Scope block: NAO COBRE parkinsonismos atipicos, tremor essencial, outras sindromes de rigidez nao-Parkinson
- **LEVODOPA TIMING RULE prominently declared here:** Registration of medication schedule mandatory at admission. All sessions scheduled 45-60 min post-dose.

**Section 2 — Avaliacao Inicial Mandatoria:**
- Bateria universal herdada: TUG + SPPB-BR + Dinamometria Manual
- BBS-BR — mandatory (postural instability is cardinal)
- FES-I-BR — mandatory (freezing increases fall fear)
- MoCA-BR — mandatory (cognitive fluctuations common in PD)
- MDS-UPDRS Parte III Motor (Goulart 2004 — BR validation) — measured in standardized "on" state; document time post-dose
- Escala de Hoehn and Yahr Modificada (Goulart 2004) — clinical staging for exercise selection
- Registro de horarios de medicacao dopaminergica — mandatory scheduling tool

**Section 3 — Alinhamento com Diretrizes:**
- APTA CPG 2022 (PubMed 34963139; PMC9046970) — primary guideline
- PMC11307027 (2024) — levodopa-exercise interaction evidence
- Oxford CEBM classification

**Section 4 — Estrutura de Fases (3 functional phases):**
- ALL phases: sessions in "on" state (45-60 min post-dose) is structural rule
- Phase transitions use function-based criteria with "at least 3 sessions in confirmed on state" as additional P-06 entry criterion for Phase 2
- Include Hoehn and Yahr staging guidance for expected trajectory

**Sections 5-7 — Phase content:**
Each phase section MUST include: entry/exit criteria, classified interventions, two-column dosage tables with 3 frailty rows, on/off state management.

Phase-specific content:
- Phase 1 (Section 5): Levodopa timing protocol (full table from research — on vs off state differentiation for prescription). External cueing introduction (visual, auditory, tactile). Freezing management strategies. Static balance (P-07 Section 7.2 Level 1). Session safety (P-07 Section 5.2). "Off state alternative session" protocol: reduce load 30%, seated exercises, stretching, breathing, NO balance hierarchy progression.
- Phase 2 (Section 6): High-intensity resistance training per APTA CPG 2022. Dynamic balance (P-07 Section 7.2 Level 2). Amplitude-focused training (LSVT BIG principles). Resistance dosing cross-reference P-08 Section 6.3. CAREGIVER TRAINING SESSION 1 and 2 (formal component — see caregiver training section below).
- Phase 3 (Section 7): Dual-task training — reference P-03 Section 7 TEMPLATE CLUSTER NEUROMOTOR with Parkinson adaptation: "on" state mandatory for DT, reduce complexity if approaching off. Community reintegration. HEP with external cueing strategies. CAREGIVER TRAINING SESSION 3 (review + HEP supervision).

**CAREGIVER TRAINING — Formal Protocol Component (APTA CPG 2022):**
Embed within the phase sections (not as appendix). Minimum 3 sessions:
- Phase 1: 1 orientation session (30 min) — recognize on/off states, basic safety
- Phase 2: 1 practical session (45 min) — external cueing techniques for freezing, safe gait support without "carrying"
- Phase 3: 1 review session (30 min) — supervised HEP by caregiver, recognize decline signs
Content: recognize on vs off (rigidity, bradykinesia, facial expression), cueing for freezing (visual line, auditory counting, tactile touch), correct support positioning (beside not in front), home environmental safety (rugs, lighting, grab bars), when to call PT vs emergency

**Section 8 — Monitoramento de Dor:**
- NRS 0-10 standard (verbal communication typically preserved in PD)
- Note: if cognitive fluctuations severe (MoCA < 18), consider PAINAD-BR supplementarily

**Section 9 — Bandeiras Vermelhas:**

- P-06 specific: akinetic crisis, extreme rigidity with hyperthermia (neuroleptic malignant-like syndrome), acute intense confusion (differentiate from severe off state), falls with injury, sudden worsening not explained by medication change

**Section 10 — Interacoes com Polifarmacia:**

- Dopamine agonists (orthostatic hypotension — position change caution)

**Section 12 — Indicadores de Desfecho:**

## P-18 DEMENCIA — Section-by-section requirements:

**Section 1 — Criterios Diagnosticos e de Inclusao:**
- Demencia / Alzheimer leve a moderado
- MMSE >= 10 ou CDR 0.5-2
- Cuidador ou familiar identificado (mandatory — no protocol without identified caregiver)
- Exclusao: demencia grave (MMSE < 10 ou CDR 3), agitacao psicomotora grave nao controlada, recusa persistente de participacao
- Scope block: NAO COBRE demencia grave terminal (CDR 3), delirium agudo, comprometimento cognitivo leve sem diagnostico de demencia

**Section 2 — Avaliacao Inicial Mandatoria:**
- Bateria universal herdada: TUG + SPPB-BR + Dinamometria Manual
- BBS-BR + FES-I-BR — mandatory
- MoCA-BR (Memoria 2013; PubMed 22368034) — mandatory cognitive screening
- MMSE-BR (Brucki 2003; education-adjusted cutoffs PubMed 29213658) — mandatory; primary stratification tool for instruction complexity and HEP policy
- PAINAD-BR (Morello 2015; PubMed 25993063) — BASELINE pain assessment for non-verbal patients
- CDR (Clinical Dementia Rating) — diagnostic context staging
- Caregiver assessment — identify primary caregiver, assess caregiver burden, establish communication channel

**Section 3 — Alinhamento com Diretrizes:**
- NICE NG97 (Dementia: Assessment, Management and Support)
- PMC12599043 (2025) — meta-analysis: multimodal exercise >= 12 weeks improves cognition (MMSE d=0.30, MoCA d=0.39)
- Kalbe E et al. 2007 — implicit/procedural learning preserved in Alzheimer (23 experimental studies)
- Oxford CEBM classification

**Section 4 — Estrutura de Fases:**
- ALL phases use procedural/implicit learning instruction design
- Phase transitions include "caregiver trained — HEP checklist completed" as additional criterion
- MMSE/MoCA stratification determines instruction complexity at EVERY phase

**Sections 5-7 — Phase content:**
Each phase: entry/exit criteria, classified interventions, dosage tables with frailty rows, cognitive-adapted instruction.

**COGNITIVE-ADAPTED INSTRUCTION SYSTEM (embedded in ALL phase sections):**
This is the central pedagogical system of P-18. Include the full stratification table:
- MMSE >= 24: verbal + demonstration; repeat simple instruction; HEP up to 5 exercises with image+text sheet
- MMSE 20-23: demonstration primary; verbal reduced to 1 command; repeat each exercise; HEP max 3 exercises with pictographic sheet (image + exercise name)
- MMSE < 24 (mandatory cutoff): procedural/implicit instruction: hand-over-hand, vanishing cues, mirroring; NO multi-step verbal instruction; HEP 2-3 exercises max with pictographic sheet (image + frequency symbol only)
- MMSE < 18: hand-over-hand; functional context tasks (real chair sit-to-stand, real cup reaching); HEP 2 exercises only; caregiver present in ALL sessions

Include procedural/implicit instruction techniques: direct demonstration, hand-over-hand, vanishing cues, context-embedded tasks, music and rhythm, consistent repetition (SAME sequence, SAME environment, SAME therapist).

Phase-specific content:
- Phase 1 (Section 5): Assessment and instruction adaptation. Establish routine (same time, same place, same sequence). Functional exercises embedded in context. Static balance (P-07 Level 1). Session safety (P-07 Section 5.2). Caregiver orientation session.
- Phase 2 (Section 6): Progressive functional load with maintained instruction consistency. Dynamic balance (P-07 Level 2). Resistance per P-08 Section 6.3. Music-assisted exercises. Caregiver practical session.
- Phase 3 (Section 7): Dual-task training — reference P-03 Section 7 TEMPLATE CLUSTER NEUROMOTOR with dementia adaptation: cognitive task must be FAMILIAR and PROCEDURAL (e.g., sing known song); NEVER use arithmetic or verbal fluency for MMSE < 20. Community-functional tasks. HEP finalization with caregiver. CAREGIVER review session.

**PICTOGRAPHIC HEP POLICY (formal section within Phase 3 or as subsection):**
- MMSE < 24 = pictographic sheet MANDATORY; do NOT deliver text-based HEP
- Image = clear photograph or drawing of exercise
- Frequency symbol = clock / suns / day-of-week icons
- Caregiver receives copy and is trained in instruction technique
- Cite Kalbe 2007 as foundation (procedural learning preserved)

**Section 8 — Monitoramento de Dor:**
- PAINAD-BR as PRIMARY instrument when MMSE < 18 or verbal communication compromised
- NRS as supplementary when patient can indicate intensity with visual support (faces scale or card-assisted numeric)
- Include PAINAD 5-domain table and action thresholds
- Abbey Pain Scale: mention as alternative with LOW confidence note ("validacao cultural BR nao confirmada — preferir PAINAD-BR")

**Section 9 — Bandeiras Vermelhas:**
- Inherited general red flags
- P-18 specific: intense psychomotor agitation without apparent cause, fall with injury, food/water refusal (acute decline), sudden cognitive deterioration beyond expected trajectory, caregiver burnout signs

**Section 10 — Interacoes com Polifarmacia:**
- Cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine) — nausea, exercise timing
- Memantine — generally well-tolerated; monitor dizziness
- Atypical antipsychotics — sedation; schedule session away from peak
- Benzodiazepines — AGS Beers potentially inappropriate; fall risk; advocate deprescription discussion
- STOPPFall reference

**Section 11 — Precaucoes Cardiorrespiratorias (inherited)**

**Section 12 — Indicadores de Desfecho:**
- MMSE-BR, MoCA-BR (when applicable), TUG, BBS-BR, PAINAD-BR (pain), SPPB-BR
- Note: cognitive measures (MMSE/MoCA) are not expected to improve dramatically — functional outcomes (TUG, BBS, SPPB) are primary indicators of protocol effectiveness
